Origin Story

The $1 that
crossed the border.

Guanajuato, Mexico

It was a Tuesday night. June 14, 1989. I was 6 years old. My dad handed me $1 to calm me down as we crossed. I took it. And off we went.

Born in Guanajuato, Mexico. Raised in South Central Los Angeles. Oldest of 5 brothers. LA Riots. Poverty. Bike stolen at gunpoint — twice.

Joined the US Navy at 18 — two months before 9/11. Deployed to Iraq in 2010. Came back and kept going.

Interned at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory. Broke my hand. My mentor told me I could quit. I finished anyway.

First in my family to earn a college degree — UC Irvine, Electrical Engineering 2016. Then a Master's from Johns Hopkins 2022.

Now I'm an Engineering Manager in Space Systems. And I'm teaching everything I learned about money, systems, and finishing — to the people who need it most.

An engineer doesn't just manage systems — he builds them. PoolGuardian is an FPGA-based embedded safety system designed to detect when a person falls into a swimming pool and trigger an immediate alarm.

◆ Why FPGA?
Chosen for its ability to process multiple sensor inputs simultaneously in real time. Motion alone, or water alone, never triggers the alarm — this two-factor logic separates a real fall from a splash, a ball, or rain.
